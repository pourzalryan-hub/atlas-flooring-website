# Lead Generation Audit — Atlas Rug & Design Centre

Prepared June 2026. Scope: conversion-rate optimization (CRO) review of the highest-intent pages on atlasrugflooring.com following the Phase 2 SEO build. Every page already carries the global CTABanner (gold band: "Get a Free Quote" → /contact, "Call (416) 533-3366" → tel:, plus address/hours line). Recommendations below assume that baseline and focus on what's missing *above* it, where buying decisions actually happen.

Standing context for all pages:
- Phone: (416) 533-3366. Showroom: 978 Bathurst St, Mon–Sat 10–4, other times by appointment.
- The single biggest sitewide gap: **the phone number and a quote button do not appear until the bottom CTABanner.** High-intent visitors who land mid-funnel have to scroll the whole page to convert. A sticky mobile call/quote bar would lift conversions on every page below.
- The /contact form is the only conversion endpoint. There is no inline "request a quote" form on any service page — every CTA bounces the user to /contact, adding a step.

## Quick Wins (implement in next sprint)

1. **Sticky mobile call/quote bar** on every page (fixed bottom: "Call" + "Free Quote"). Most flooring traffic is mobile and high-intent; this is the single biggest lift available.
2. **Add a mid-page CTA band** to all 11 service pages immediately after the primary product/decision section — don't make buyers reach the footer to convert.
3. **Price anchoring** on /hardwood, /laminate, /vinyl, /carpet — installed $/sq ft ranges. Price-screening shoppers leave silently when no number appears.
4. **Pre-fill the /contact service dropdown** via query param (e.g. /contact?service=hardwood) from each service page CTA — removes a step and tags the lead.
5. **Photo-quote offer on /stair-runners:** "Text a photo of your stairs to (416) 533-3366 for a same-day ballpark." Highest-converting offer for that category.
6. **Add CTAs to /projects and /projects/[slug]** — the proof pages currently dead-end.
7. **Swap the generic "Satisfaction Guaranteed" trust block on location pages for the WhyAtlas component** for stronger, specific E-E-A-T.
8. **Response-time promise on /contact** ("within one business day") and equal-weight phone option.

## Medium-Term Improvements

1. **Inline quote forms** on top service pages (stair-runners, hardwood, commercial) so high-intent users convert without leaving the page. Commercial needs its own B2B form (company, building type, sq ft, timeline).
2. **Per-project CTA + ReviewsSection** on /projects/[slug], pulling service type and area from project data ("Want a [type] floor like this in [area]?").
3. **Category quick-selectors** (wool vs nylon on carpet; AC-rating on laminate; species on hardwood) to self-qualify leads and shorten sales calls.
4. **Condo board checklist box** on /condo-flooring (IIC underlay, board approval, elevator booking, COI) — converts anxious condo owners.
5. **Geo-specific proof + project features on location pages** now that 58 projects span the neighbourhoods — wire each location to its real local projects first.
6. **Comparison-guide soft CTAs + sample-PDF email capture** to convert top-of-funnel /compare traffic.
7. **Commercial line-card / spec sheet download** gated by email for property managers and procurement.

## Page-Level CRO Notes

- **Conversion endpoint is a bottleneck:** every CTA routes to /contact. Reducing that to a one-click pre-tagged form (or inline form) is the highest-ROI structural change after the sticky bar.
- **Trust assets are underused above the fold.** WhyAtlas and ReviewsSection now exist; push the strongest signal (family-owned since 1959, own crews, no subcontractors) higher on revenue pages, not just near the footer.
- **Local proof is now available and should be deployed:** 58 project pages across neighbourhoods give every location and service page concrete, linkable evidence. Currently underexploited on location pages.
- **Phone-first buyers are common in this trade.** Treat the phone number as a co-equal CTA, not a secondary one, especially on mobile and on /contact.
- **Comparison pages are SEO + funnel-entry assets.** Make sure each ends with both a "we'll bring samples" soft CTA and a hard quote CTA, and that the relevant service pages link back to them.
