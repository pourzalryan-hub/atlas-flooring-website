## SECTION 4 — CORE TECHNOLOGY

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Understanding Vinyl Core Types

**3-column feature cards:**

**SPC — Stone Plastic Composite**  
*Most popular*  
Ultra-rigid core made from limestone and PVC. The most durable and dimensionally stable option — doesn't expand/contract significantly with temperature. 100% waterproof. Best for high-traffic and commercial-grade residential. Slightly harder underfoot.  
**Best for:** Basements, kitchens, high-traffic areas

**WPC — Wood Plastic Composite**  
Foam core creates a softer, warmer underfoot feel compared to SPC. Also 100% waterproof. Slightly less rigid than SPC. Excellent acoustic properties — quieter underfoot.  
**Best for:** Bedrooms, living rooms, comfort-first spaces

**Standard Vinyl (LVP)**  
Traditional 4mm–6mm vinyl without a rigid core. More budget-accessible. Less tolerant of subfloor imperfections and temperature swings. Suitable for controlled environments.  
**Best for:** Lower-traffic areas, rental properties, budget installs

## SECTION 6 — WEAR LAYER GUIDE

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 34px):** Wear Layer — Don't Overlook This Number

**Body:**
> The wear layer is the transparent protective coating on top of the vinyl. It's measured in **mils (thousandths of an inch)** and determines how well your floor resists scratches, scuffs, and staining.

| Wear Layer | Durability | Best For |
|------------|------------|----------|
| 6 mil | Light residential | Guest rooms, low-traffic areas |
| 8 mil | Standard residential | Bedrooms, living rooms |
| 12 mil | Heavy residential | Main floors, families, pets |
| 20 mil | Commercial grade | High traffic, rentals, commercial use |

**Recommendation:** We suggest **12 mil minimum** for any Toronto home with pets, kids, or regular foot traffic.

## SECTION 7 — INSTALLATION

**Background:** White  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Professional Vinyl Installation

**Three install methods (icon + title + description):**

**Floating (Click-Lock)**  
Planks or tiles snap together and float over the subfloor on an underpad. No glue, no nails. Easiest to replace individual planks if damaged. Suitable for most residential applications.

**Glue-Down**  
Vinyl is adhered directly to the subfloor using pressure-sensitive adhesive. More stable, recommended for commercial settings or slabs. Harder to remove later.

**Loose Lay**  
Friction-based installation — no adhesive, no click mechanism. Fast to install and easy to replace. Best for flat, smooth subfloors and commercial spaces.

**Note:** Atlas installers will recommend the correct method for your specific subfloor, space, and product.

**4-step process:**

1. **Subfloor Assessment** — Check for level, moisture, and soundness.
2. **Subfloor Prep** — Fill dips, sand humps, address any moisture concerns.
3. **Vinyl Installation** — Click-lock, glue-down, or loose lay as appropriate.
4. **Trim & Transitions** — Baseboards, threshold strips, and door transitions finished cleanly.

## SECTION 10 — FAQ

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Luxury Vinyl Questions, Answered

**Accordion:**

**Q: Is luxury vinyl really waterproof?**  
A: Yes — the vinyl itself is 100% waterproof. The key is ensuring joints are properly installed so water can't penetrate to the subfloor. Our installation ensures this.

**Q: Can luxury vinyl go over existing flooring?**  
A: Often yes, if the existing floor is flat, structurally sound, and not too high. We'll assess during the consultation.

**Q: Is luxury vinyl good for radiant heat?**  
A: Most SPC products are compatible with radiant heat up to 27°C (80°F). Confirm the specific product you choose during your consultation.

**Q: How does luxury vinyl compare to laminate?**  
A: LVP is 100% waterproof; laminate is not (unless WPC core). LVP is generally more suitable for basements and kitchens. Laminate can feel more premium underfoot in dry above-grade areas. We carry both — come see them side by side.

**Q: How long does luxury vinyl last?**  
A: Quality LVP with a 12mil+ wear layer should last 20–25 years with normal residential use. Many products carry 25–30 year manufacturer warranties.
