## SECTION 4 — PRODUCT FEATURES

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** What to Look For in Laminate

**Feature table / two-column content:**

**AC Rating (Wear Layer)**
| AC Rating | Use Class | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| AC3 | Moderate residential | Low-traffic rooms |
| AC4 | General residential | All residential — recommended minimum |
| AC5 | Heavy commercial | High-traffic homes, rentals, studios |

We recommend **AC4 minimum** for all residential installs. For rentals or heavy-use spaces, AC5.

**Thickness**  
> 8mm is entry-level. **12mm is the sweet spot** for a solid, realistic underfoot feel. 14–15mm is premium — noticeably quieter and more rigid. Thicker also tolerates minor subfloor irregularities better.

**Core Type**  
> Standard HDF (High-Density Fibreboard) is suitable for above-grade installs. **Waterproof WPC/SPC core** options are available for basements and bathrooms — ask about these during your consultation.

**Texture / Embossing**  
> Smooth: dated look. Embossed-in-register (EIR): texture follows the printed grain — the gold standard for realism. Always ask for EIR.

## SECTION 6 — INSTALLATION

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Professional Laminate Installation

**Body:**
> Laminate is a floating floor — it's not glued or nailed to the subfloor. The planks click together and rest on an underpad. This makes it one of the faster installs, but proper subfloor preparation is critical — any bumps, dips, or moisture issues will show up in the finished floor.
>
> Our installation team handles full subfloor assessment, prep, levelling where needed, underpad installation, and the laminate itself — ensuring a flat, stable, professional result.

**4-step quick process:**

1. **Subfloor Inspection** — We check for moisture, level, and soundness. Issues are corrected before a single plank goes down.
2. **Underpad Installation** — Quality underpad is installed for acoustic insulation and subfloor protection.
3. **Plank Installation** — Click-lock installation, staggered joints, clean transitions.
4. **Trim & Finishing** — Baseboards, thresholds, and transitions installed and caulked.

## SECTION 8 — FAQ

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Laminate Questions, Answered

**Accordion:**

**Q: Can laminate be installed in a basement?**  
A: Standard laminate is not recommended for basements due to moisture. However, we carry WPC (Wood Plastic Composite) core laminate that is waterproof and suitable below grade. Ask us about these options.

**Q: Can laminate be installed over existing floors?**  
A: Often yes — laminate can float over most existing hard floors if they're level, structurally sound, and not too high (watch for door clearances). We'll assess your existing floor during consultation.

**Q: How long does laminate last?**  
A: Quality AC4/AC5 laminate with proper care typically lasts 15–25 years. It cannot be refinished, so when it's worn, it's replaced — but modern products are very long-lasting.

**Q: Is laminate suitable for pets?**  
A: Yes — scratch-resistant laminate (AC4+) handles pet nails well. It's also easy to clean. The main watch-out is pet accidents — prolonged moisture exposure can swell the joints. Clean spills quickly.

**Q: Does laminate feel hollow underfoot?**  
A: Thinner, cheaper laminate can sound hollow. Our 12mm+ options with quality underpad have a substantially more solid feel. Come feel the difference in our showroom.

**Q: Does laminate need to acclimate?**  
A: Yes — laminate should acclimate in the installation room for 48 hours before install, allowing it to adjust to your home's temperature and humidity.
