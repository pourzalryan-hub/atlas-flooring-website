## SECTION 4 — MATERIALS

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Runner Materials We Carry

**4-column grid (icon + title + description):**

**Wool**  
Naturally resilient, soft, and luxurious. Wool runner fibres recover from foot traffic exceptionally well and hold pattern and colour for decades. The premium choice for traditional and heritage homes.

**Nylon**  
The most durable synthetic option. Nylon runners resist crushing and staining, making them ideal for high-traffic staircases in active households. Available in hundreds of styles.

**Polypropylene / Olefin**  
Budget-friendly and moisture-resistant. Great for painted stairs or less formal staircases. Wide range of patterns available at accessible price points.

**Natural Fibres (Sisal / Jute / Seagrass)**  
An earthy, organic aesthetic that works beautifully in coastal, farmhouse, and contemporary spaces. Note: natural fibres are harder to clean — best for lower-traffic stairs.

## SECTION 5 — INSTALLATION OPTIONS

**Background:** `#1C1C1C`  
**Layout:** Two-column — left: content, right: close-up image of runner installation detail  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 40px, white):** How We Install Stair Runners

**Three installation methods (gold icon + bold title + description, white/grey text):**

**Waterfall Method**  
The runner drapes over each tread edge and drops straight down each riser. Creates a flowing, continuous look. The most common method — clean and timeless.

**Hollywood / Wrapped Method**  
The runner wraps tightly around each tread nosing, following the contour of the stair. Creates a more formal, tailored appearance — popular in traditional and estate homes.

**Stair Rod Finish**  
A decorative brass, nickel, or bronze rod is installed at each tread/riser junction, holding the runner in place decoratively. Adds a classic, formal touch — popular in Victorian and Edwardian homes.

**Sub-note (Lato, 15px, #C8C8C8):**
> All runners are secured with tack strips and specialty staples for a firm, safe installation. We never use adhesive alone — which can cause bunching over time.

## SECTION 6 — SIZING GUIDE

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 36px, charcoal):** Stair Runner Sizing — What to Know

**Body (Lato, 17px):**
> Standard stair runners are typically **27 inches** or **32 inches** wide, leaving exposed wood on either side. The amount of exposed tread you show is a design choice — wider runners look more substantial, narrower runners show off more of your hardwood.
>
> We measure every staircase precisely and cut all runners in-house — ensuring perfect pattern alignment and zero guesswork. We also account for pattern repeats so that nothing is cut mid-motif.

**Simple visual guide (3 illustrations or diagrams):**
- Narrow runner (27") — 4" exposed tread on each side
- Standard runner (32") — 2.5" exposed on each side  
- Wide runner (36") — minimal exposure for a fuller look

## SECTION 8 — FAQ

**Background:** `#FAF8F3`  
**H2 (Playfair Display, 38px):** Stair Runner Questions, Answered

**Accordion:**

**Q: Do I need to have hardwood stairs for a runner?**  
A: Not at all. We install runners on hardwood, painted wood, and even concrete stairs. For painted stairs, we'll ensure the installation method protects the paint.

**Q: Can I choose my own pattern?**  
A: Absolutely. Our showroom carries hundreds of patterns, and our team can also help you source custom or special-order designs if you have something specific in mind.

**Q: How wide should my stair runner be?**  
A: This depends on your stair width and personal taste. We typically recommend 27"–32" for standard staircases, leaving 2–4" of tread visible on each side. We'll show you options during the consultation.

**Q: How long does installation take?**  
A: A typical single-flight staircase (12–14 stairs) takes half a day. L-shaped or double-flight staircases may take a full day.

**Q: Are stair runners safe?**  
A: Yes — professionally installed runners are held firmly by tack strips and fasteners, making stairs safer (more grip) compared to bare hardwood, especially for children and elderly family members.

**Q: How do I clean my stair runner?**  
A: Vacuum weekly. Treat spills immediately with a clean cloth and appropriate fibre-safe cleaner. Professional cleaning is recommended every 12–18 months.
